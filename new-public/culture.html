Cultural Initiatives

Robotic workforces change how people envision their purpose.

Contemporary Narrative Shaping

Our founder has spent over a decade in Hollywood, building the network of people who believe robotic workforces are the key to the future.

Talented executive producers like Julia Pierrepoint  and Manu Gargi bring financing expertise and networking.

Lt. General Steven L. Kwast of the Air Force brings a solid understanding of using cultural domains to drive technical and policy innovation.

Our Fables Initiative explored demographic trends of several nations to predict their next generation's media consumption.  We believe national actors will unify their private and public sectors to stake claims in space exploration and asteroid mining, prompting investments in the creation of pro-robotic workforce content.

Counterculture Outreach

Emblem 21 has spearheaded multiple outreach attempts to influence counterculture evolution.

We've utilized esoteric quasi-religious blogs to politically mobilize 4chan in 2011 and in 2013. Our efforts helped secure Steve Bannon's involvement in GamerGate and we were able to mobilize gamers to undermine Gawker's digital ad revenue.

Our IDOL project explores how to merge cryptocurrency with content creators to bypass political and financial censorship by channeling Ethereum liquidity into content production and distribution.

We're currently experimenting with metadata insurgency techniques to reduce the efficacy of automated censorship against our content.

High-Impact Robotic Workforce Content

Emblem 21 started as a book called, "The Empath", which explored the geopolitical and economic outcomes of a world where the financial sector controlled Singularity-level artificial intelligence.   This book has been turned into a TV show pitch called "Grain of Truth" and the quantitative entertainment firm we created for the show attracted the attention of Caroline-Sarah de Rothschild.
