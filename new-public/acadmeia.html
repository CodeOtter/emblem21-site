Academia Initiatives

Robotic workforces demand research pipelines for multiple domains.

Psychographics and Memetics

Modernizing psychology, sociology, and communication with data science will be essential to help populations understand and utilize the opportunities robotic labor provides. Calvin Abbasi, a communications professor at CSUN, has offered to assist in this endeavor.

Economics and Finance

Robotic workforces can operate anywhere in the world, allowing an on-demand solution for labor shortages.  Instead of migrants moving across borders, workers can stay in their homelands and cycle currency between countries at a higher velocity.  An abundance of robotic labor allows asteroid mining to flourish at accessible costs for investors throughout the world.

Political Science

What does democracy look like when workforces are an on-demand blend of citizenry status?  How does the authority sector regulate robotic workforce usage?  What kind of legal reforms are needed to find the right balance between security and productivity?  Robotic workforces help reform political science into a field for data-driven policy guidance.
